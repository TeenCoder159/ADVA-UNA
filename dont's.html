Dont's

Don't take aspirin, ibuprofen, or other NSAIDs which may increase bleeding risk
Don't self-medicate with antibiotics (they don't work against dengue virus)
Don't delay seeking medical attention if symptoms worsen
Don't ignore warning signs like severe abdominal pain or bleeding
Don't consume alcoholic beverages
Don't smoke or use tobacco products
Don't engage in strenuous physical activities
Don't leave skin exposed unnecessarily (wear long sleeves when possible)
Don't skip meals completely, even with poor appetite
Don't forget to take prescribed medications
Don't ignore the need for hydratio
Don't miss follow-up blood tests as recommended
Don't allow water to stagnate in and around your home
Don't return to work/school prematurely
Don't consume spicy or heavily processed foods that are hard to digest
Don't take any supplements without consulting your doctor
Don't ignore persistent symptoms even after fever subsides
Don't stay in areas with high mosquito populations if avoidable
Don't share personal items that may have blood contact (toothbrushes, razors)
Don't forget to inform healthcare providers about being diagnosed with dengue
Don't stop treatment without medical advice
Don't ignore the need for rest, even if you start feeling better
Don't consume caffeinated beverages in excess as they can contribute to dehydration
Don't take hot showers during high fever periods
Don't allow mosquitoes to breed in your environment by leaving containers with water open
