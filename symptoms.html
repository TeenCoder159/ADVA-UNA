Symptoms to Evaluate for Potential Dengue Infection

High Fever
High fever (typically 104°F/40°C) that comes on suddenly. This is usually the first and most common symptom of dengue infection.
Usually lasts 2-7 days
Severe Headache
Severe headache, particularly behind the eyes. This is often described as a persistent, throbbing pain that worsens with eye movement.
May not respond well to regular painkillers
Joint and Muscle Pain
Joint and muscle pain (hence "breakbone fever"). The pain can be so severe that it feels like bones are breaking.
Often affects back, arms, legs, and joints
Skin Rash
Widespread skin rash that typically appears 3-4 days after the onset of fever. It often starts on the trunk and spreads to extremities.
May be itchy and red with small bumps
Digestive Issues
Nausea and vomiting are common symptoms that can lead to dehydration and electrolyte imbalances if severe.
May worsen as disease progresses
Loss of Appetite
Loss of appetite is common and can contribute to weakness and fatigue during the illness.
Can lead to significant weight loss
Fatigue and Weakness
Fatigue and weakness that can be severe and persist even after other symptoms have resolved.
May last weeks after acute infection
Mild Bleeding
Mild bleeding from nose or gums due to decreased platelet count and increased capillary fragility.
More common in severe dengue
Easy Bruising
Easy bruising even with minimal trauma, indicating platelet dysfunction or decreased platelet count.
Check for unexplained bruises
Severe Abdominal Pain
Severe abdominal pain that is persistent or increasing in intensity. This is a warning sign of severe dengue.
Requires immediate medical attention
Persistent Vomiting
Persistent vomiting that prevents adequate oral intake. This can lead to dehydration and is a warning sign.
3 or more episodes in 24 hours is concerning
Difficulty Breathing
Difficulty breathing or rapid breathing may indicate fluid accumulation in the lungs or respiratory distress.
Medical emergency requiring immediate care
